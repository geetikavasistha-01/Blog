// the geeky kunoichi

I'm a Computer Science & Machine Learning student who spends most days somewhere between writing code, untangling distributed systems, and trying to make sense of data that doesn't want to make sense.

My world revolves around data science, high-performance backend systems (Go, Python, Rust — pick your poison), and climate tech — building things that can sense, predict, and respond to a planet that's changing faster than our infrastructure is. Solar forecasting, heat intelligence, smarter grids — the unglamorous, deeply necessary stuff.

This blog is where I document all of it: build logs, half-finished architecture diagrams, the bugs that took three days and the fixes that took three minutes, and the occasional spiral into engineering philosophy at 2am.

The name Kunoichi — a female shinobi — felt right. Stealth, precision, adaptability, constant refinement. In tech and AI spaces that weren't always built with us in mind, that's the energy I try to bring: quietly competent, always sharpening something.
